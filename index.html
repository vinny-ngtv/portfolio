Fruit Project

My still life project focused on fruits, and how the intensity of their colours can be manipulated to create brighter and bolder paintings. I had researched the work of Amy Whitehouse and Kaiti Papadimitriou to inspire my two outcomes. My final pieces show a pomegranate, painted from a photo I took and digitally edited myself as a reference. The first outcome was made with acrylic paint and my second mixed media.

Modelling and Observational Sketching

I wanted to develop my modelling skills as well as my observational drawing, so I created models out of card to investigate the effects of light and shadows. It was interesting to see the shadows change form as the light source moved. Following that, I tried making sketches of the models from life using a ball-point pen and cross-hatching technique.

Digital Art

In my free time, I enjoy making digital art of fictional characters. Using a tablet and stylus, I like to create characters based on colour schemes, and also illustrate existing characters from fictional worlds. Sometimes I share my digital art on social media for others to see.

Architecture Modelling

I had completed the Bronze Arts Award where the aim was to create our own architectural models of follies using card, accompanied by orthographic drawings of our designs. I started making initial models to explore the capabilities of card, then I chose two of my models to combine and develop into a final design.
